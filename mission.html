The Scottsboro Boys

Arrested in 1931, nine teenagers were forced into a sham trial without counsel. Their case traveled to the U.S. Supreme Court in Powell v. Alabama (1932), a landmark ruling that established the right to a lawyer as a fundamental pillar of Due Process.

"The system refused to yield. It took 82 years for the State of Alabama to grant posthumous pardons in 2013, finally acknowledging their innocence after nearly a century of stolen lives."
